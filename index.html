Gardenias & Gunpowder

Southern Gothic Novel by Dawn Marie Daras

About the Story

I wrote Gardenias & Gunpowder to explore the tension between personal identity and the demands of love and duty. The novel follows Andrea, a woman leaving the tightly scripted world of her Mormon upbringing, only to become entangled in two mirror‑polished relationships: a mother whose love reflects only herself, and a husband whose devotion to his mission leaves little room for anyone else.

When Andrea marries a good‑hearted cop, she believes she has found her partner. But his badge hides a covert life — a life that vanishes as quickly as corrupted logs in a secure system. As the uniforms fade and the badge disappears, Andrea learns to become her own shield: her inner strength, the protection that truly matters. This story draws from some of my own experiences as the wife of a police lieutenant in the South.

She wrote back one sentence:
"I'm proud of your work as a Southern cop."

That was all she could say.
Because she was proud. Of what he had done. Of what it cost.
But pride and love weren't the same thing.
And grief — that particular grief — wasn't about death.
It was about choice.
He had chosen to be a shield.
And she had needed a partner.
That's the secret of so many women in law enforcement families — those who stay, and those who leave: they lose their husbands not to bullets, but to silence; not to danger, but to distance; to the weight of mission; to the loneliness of being married to someone who serves a cause so completely, there's nothing left for home.

This is also a story about family secrets — how Andrea tries to heal from growing up with a society mother struggling with alcoholism, whose perfection is only skin‑deep. She believes she has married a man who is a paragon in their town, only to discover he is living a covert life undercover. Andrea finds herself repeating familiar patterns of secrecy, just as she did in her family. Ultimately, it is a story about how what we don't heal, we repeat. Told with Southern charm, humor, and dry wit.
